Integrating Sophía with ecOS & LTM¶

Where Sophía Fits¶

  • In ecOS (Shell): Operators create labels, rationales, policies, and run simulations. Successful artifacts are packaged for minting.
  • In LTM (Model Layer): Telemetry normalization, feature generation, and historical feedback quantify artifact utility over time.
  • In the Ledger: NFT minting, version lineage, and payout contracts manage attribution and value distribution.

Example Lifecycle¶

  1. Create — Analyst proposes a policy in ecOS and explains rationale.
  2. Validate — SynA evaluates the policy against historical telemetry; evidence/uncertainty are recorded.
  3. Mint — The artifact is minted as an NFT (v1.0) with contributor attribution.
  4. Activate — Policy is deployed with guardrails; ongoing outcomes update utility metrics.
  5. Compensate — Usage fees and royalties are streamed to contributor wallets based on utility and consumption.
